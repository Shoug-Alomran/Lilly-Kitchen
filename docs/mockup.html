Slow-Roasted Lamb
with Pomegranate Glaze

"A deeply fragrant dish built for slow afternoons and full tables — the kind of recipe that fills the house with warmth before it even reaches the plate."

By Lilly · March 2025 · ⭐ 4.9 (214 reviews)
⏱
Prep
15 min
🔥
Cook
2 hr
🍽
Serves
6
📊
Difficulty
Medium
🖨 Print 📤 Share
Ingredients
−
6
servings
+
For the lamb
1.8 kg bone-in lamb shoulder
4 tbsp olive oil
6 garlic cloves, minced
2 tsp ground cumin
1 tsp cinnamon
Salt & black pepper
For the glaze
200ml pomegranate molasses
2 tbsp honey
1 tbsp lemon juice
½ tsp allspice
To serve
Fresh pomegranate seeds
Handful of fresh mint
Toasted pine nuts
📋 Copy ingredients list
Method
01
Marinate the lamb. Score the lamb shoulder deeply all over with a sharp knife. Mix together the olive oil, minced garlic, cumin, cinnamon, salt and pepper into a thick paste. Rub generously into every cut and over the surface. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or ideally overnight.
02
Slow roast. Preheat your oven to 160°C (fan). Place the lamb in a deep roasting dish, add 200ml of water or stock to the bottom, cover tightly with foil and roast for 2 hours until tender enough to pull apart with a fork.
03
Make the glaze. While the lamb roasts, combine pomegranate molasses, honey, lemon juice and allspice in a small saucepan. Simmer over medium heat for 5–6 minutes until slightly thickened and glossy.
04
Glaze and finish. Remove the foil, increase oven to 200°C. Brush the lamb liberally with the pomegranate glaze. Return uncovered for 20 minutes until caramelised and deeply coloured. Rest for 15 minutes before serving.
💡 Lilly's Notes

"The secret is not to rush the resting. Those 15 minutes after the oven are where all the magic happens — the juices redistribute and every bite becomes something else entirely. Serve it on a big platter with warm flatbread and don't be shy with the pomegranate seeds."
